1. Welcome to FlipLogic AI

FlipLogic AI is a deal analysis platform built specifically for fix-and-flip real estate investors. It is designed to answer one question quickly and confidently: is this deal worth pursuing?

This guide covers everything the app does, how to use each feature, and the concepts behind the numbers. You can read it cover to cover, or jump to a section using the table of contents.

Who FlipLogic AI Is For

  • Fix-and-flip investors evaluating residential properties for renovation and resale.
  • Investors working with hard money lenders who need clean deal packets.
  • Investors managing multiple deals at once who need to keep numbers straight across properties.
  • New investors who want help thinking through scope, ARV, and offers — and experienced investors who want to move faster.

What Makes FlipLogic AI Different

Most deal calculators ask you for a single rehab number and a single ARV (after-repair value) number. Real deals do not work that way. You usually have your initial guess, what an AI walkthrough or contractor estimate suggests, and what the comps actually support.

FlipLogic AI tracks all of those sources side by side and lets you switch between them with a single tap. Every downstream number — your Maximum Allowable Offer, ROI, profit, deal score — recalculates instantly. That means you can stress-test a deal in real time without redoing your math.

What You Can Do With FlipLogic AI

  • Analyze a deal across seven specialized tabs: Deal Analysis, AI Walkthrough, Comps, Rental Pivot, Stress Test, Scope of Work, and Lender Packet.
  • Pull live sale comps and rental comps from RentCast just by entering an address.
  • Walk a property with your phone — using audio, photos, video, or smart mode — and let AI generate a scope of work for you.
  • Calculate Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO) live as you type, and see at a glance how far under or over you are.
  • Score every deal as HOT, WARM, COLD, or DEAD based on your real numbers.
  • Generate a professional lender packet and a blind contractor bid sheet for printing or PDF export.
  • Save and sync deals across devices, with cloud backup.

2. Quick Start: Your First Deal in 15 Minutes

This walkthrough takes you from creating an account to having a fully analyzed deal. If you read nothing else in this guide, read this section.

Step 1 — Create Your Account

  1. Go to fliplogic.ai in any modern web browser. The app works on desktop and mobile.
  2. Enter your email and choose a password. No credit card is required.
  3. You will land on a brief onboarding screen the first time you log in. Read it or skip it — you can always reference this guide later.
New account = 5 free AI analyses

Your account starts with 5 full AI analyses on the house. You can use them on whichever deals you want, with no time limit. The trial only counts AI uses — not deals you create manually.

Step 2 — Create Your First Deal

  1. In the sidebar, tap + NEW DEAL.
  2. Enter the property address. This is the only required field to get started.
  3. Optionally enter a nickname (for example, "123 Maple Ave" or "Cleveland flip") so you can find the deal quickly later.

Step 3 — Enter Property Specs and Initial Numbers

Go to the Deal Analysis tab. Fill in what you know:

  • Property Specs: bedrooms, bathrooms, and square footage. These matter — they drive comp matching and AI cost estimates later.
  • Acquisition: purchase price, closing costs.
  • Rehab Cost — Initial Estimate: your gut guess at this point. Don't agonize over it — you'll refine it.
  • ARV — Initial Estimate: your gut number for what the property will sell for after rehab. Same idea — refine it later.

As soon as those four numbers are in, you have a working deal. The Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO) shows up live, and so does the Deal Score.

Step 4 — Pull Comps

Go to the Comps tab. Tap the RentCast button to auto-pull sale comps for the property's address.

RentCast returns sale comps, an estimated ARV, and the data you need to weight them. The first time you pull comps for a deal, FlipLogic auto-switches your active ARV source from "Initial Estimate" to "Comp-Derived" and shows a confirmation toast. That means your MAO and ROI are now driven by real comp data instead of your initial guess.

One pull per deal during beta

To protect shared API credits during the beta period, RentCast can be pulled once per deal. Make sure you've entered Property Specs (bed, bath, sqft) before pulling — that's how the comps get matched accurately.

Step 5 — Run an AI Walkthrough (Optional but Recommended)

Go to the AI Walkthrough tab. Pick the mode that fits your situation:

  • Photos — upload pictures of the property. Best when you're not on-site.
  • Audio — record yourself walking the property and describing what you see. Best when your hands are full.
  • Video — record video of the property. Best when visuals matter more than your narration.
  • Smart Mode — audio with on-demand video bursts. Best for thorough walkthroughs.

After analysis (typically 20–30 seconds), the AI returns a list of findings — things like "replace flooring throughout (~$X)" or "update kitchen cabinets (~$Y)." Push the findings you agree with into your Scope of Work with one tap.

The first AI walkthrough on a deal auto-switches your active Rehab source from "Initial Estimate" to "AI Walkthrough." Now your MAO and Deal Score are running on real, line-item data.

Step 6 — Review the Deal

Back on the Deal Analysis tab, look at the four things that matter most:

  • MAO vs. Purchase Price — green means you're under MAO (good). Red means you're over (caution).
  • Deal Score — HOT, WARM, COLD, or DEAD based on ROI, LTV, and profit.
  • Net Profit — what you walk away with after closing, holding, and selling costs.
  • ROI — return on the total project cost. 20%+ is HOT territory.

Step 7 — Generate a Lender Packet

Go to the Lender Packet tab. The app builds a professional packet from your deal data — purchase price, ARV, rehab, financials, comps, scope. Tap Print to send it to your printer or save as PDF.

That's it — you have a complete, defensible deal analysis with backing data, ready for a lender, partner, or your own files.

3. The Deal Analysis Tab

The Deal Analysis tab is the heart of FlipLogic AI. It is divided into seven sections that flow top to bottom. Numbers update live as you type.

3.1 Property Specs

Bedrooms, bathrooms, and square footage. These three numbers drive everything else — comp matching, AI cost estimates, MAO calculations.

Single source of truth

Square footage is edited only on the Deal Analysis tab. The Comps tab displays it as read-only. This prevents accidental drift between tabs.

3.2 Acquisition

  • Purchase Price — what you plan to pay for the property.
  • Closing Costs (Buy) — title, escrow, recording, etc. for the purchase side.
  • Closing Costs (Sell) — agent commissions, transfer taxes, etc. for when you resell.

3.3 Rehab Cost Breakdown

This is where FlipLogic's multi-source design shines. You can track up to three rehab cost sources at once, and pick which one drives your downstream numbers using a radio button.

Rehab SourceWhat It Means
Initial EstimateYour manual entry — your gut number.
AI WalkthroughSum of scope items generated by the AI Walkthrough feature. Updates automatically as items are added or removed.
Manual OverrideA specific number you want to lock in — e.g., a contractor's bid you've decided to trust.

Switching the active source instantly updates MAO, ROI, profit, and Deal Score. There is no "save" step — what you select is what drives the deal.

3.4 ARV Breakdown

ARV (After-Repair Value) is the price you expect the property to sell for once rehab is complete. FlipLogic tracks two ARV sources:

ARV SourceWhat It Means
Initial EstimateYour manual entry — your starting guess.
Comp-DerivedCalculated from RentCast sale comps, weighted by similarity and adjusted to your property's square footage.

As with rehab, the active ARV source drives every downstream calculation. The first RentCast pull on a deal auto-switches the active source to Comp-Derived.

3.5 Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO)

MAO is the highest price you can pay for the property and still hit your target return. The standard formula is:

MAO = (Active ARV × MAO %) − Active Rehab Cost

FlipLogic defaults to a 70% MAO percentage, which is the industry standard for fix-and-flip. You can adjust this per deal — for example, set it to 65% for a more conservative offer in a softer market, or 75% if you have other levers (low-cost capital, high-confidence ARV) that justify it.

Right next to MAO, you'll see the Purchase vs. MAO indicator:

  • Green — your purchase price is at or below MAO. The deal makes sense at the margin you want.
  • Red — your purchase price is above MAO. Either negotiate down, find more rehab efficiency, or find a higher-ARV exit.

3.6 Financing

Enter your loan terms here:

  • Loan Amount.
  • Interest Rate (annual).
  • Term (years).
  • Hold Period (months) — how long you expect to own the property before resale.

FlipLogic uses standard amortization to calculate your monthly payment, then multiplies by the hold period to get total holding costs. These flow into your net profit calculation.

3.7 Field Notes

A free-form notes section for anything that doesn't fit elsewhere — seller motivation, neighborhood quirks, agent contact, follow-up reminders. Notes save automatically and sync across your devices.

4. The AI Walkthrough Tab

The AI Walkthrough tab turns a property visit into a scope of work in about 30 seconds. Pick a mode, capture what you see, and let the AI do the line-item analysis.

4.1 The Year Built Gate

Before any AI analysis runs, you'll be asked to enter the property's year built (or explicitly skip it). This matters because:

  • Pre-1978 properties may have lead paint considerations.
  • Pre-1980 properties are more likely to have asbestos in flooring, insulation, or popcorn ceilings.
  • Older homes often have outdated electrical (knob-and-tube, aluminum) or galvanized plumbing.

Year built doesn't change the AI's process — it changes the cost estimates. A bathroom remodel in a 1955 house is not the same line item as a bathroom remodel in a 2005 house.

4.2 Photos Mode

Upload photos of the property. Useful when:

  • You couldn't visit in person and a wholesaler or agent sent you photos.
  • You want to analyze a listing's MLS photos before driving out.
  • You want a fast read on a property's general condition.

How to use it:

  1. Tap the Photos mode.
  2. Upload one or more photos (drag-and-drop on desktop, file picker on mobile).
  3. Tap Analyze. The AI examines each image and returns a list of likely scope items with rough costs.

4.3 Audio Mode

Record yourself walking the property and describing what you see. The AI transcribes your audio and generates a scope of work from your narration.

Best for:

  • On-site walkthroughs where you want to keep your hands free.
  • Conditions where you can describe more than the camera can capture (smell, sound, feel).
  • Investors who think out loud naturally.

How to use it:

  1. Tap the Audio mode.
  2. Tap Start Recording and walk the property. Describe each room — what's there, what needs work, what should stay.
  3. Tap Stop when you're done. The AI transcribes and analyzes.
Tap-to-Flag works on every device

While recording, you can tap a button to flag a moment of interest ("this kitchen needs a full gut" — flag). Flags get pulled out and emphasized in the AI's analysis. The voice trigger phrase works on desktop, but on mobile we disabled it to prevent the recording from picking up the chime — use the tap button instead.

4.4 Video Mode

Record video of the property using your rear camera. The AI captures frames every 3 seconds and analyzes them for scope items.

Best for:

  • Visual conditions that are hard to describe — wear patterns, cosmetic damage, layout.
  • Properties where you want a visual record to share with a partner or contractor later.

4.5 Smart Mode

Audio with on-demand video bursts. You record audio continuously, and tap a button when you want to capture a few seconds of video — for example, when you walk into a kitchen and want both your description and a visual. Each video burst is timestamped.

Best for thorough walkthroughs where you want the best of both audio and video without recording continuous video the whole time.

4.6 Reviewing AI Findings

After analysis, you'll see a list of findings. Each one typically has:

  • A short description (e.g., "Replace carpet in living room").
  • A category (Flooring, Kitchen, Bathroom, Mechanical, Cosmetic, etc.).
  • A rough cost estimate.

For each finding, you can:

  • Add to Scope — push it to the Scope of Work tab as a line item.
  • Edit — tweak the description or cost before adding.
  • Dismiss — ignore it (e.g., the AI flagged something you've already accounted for).

4.7 Property Spec Change Detection

If the AI hears you say things like "we'll convert this den into a fourth bedroom" or "this half-bath is going to be a full bath," it will detect the planned spec change and show a banner with three options:

  • Accept — update the property's beds/baths/sqft on the Deal Analysis tab. ARV and comps get re-weighted automatically.
  • Modify — accept with adjustments (e.g., AI heard "add 200 sqft" but you actually plan 150).
  • Dismiss — ignore the suggestion.

4.8 Recording Reliability Features

  • Wake Lock — your screen stays on automatically while recording, so the device doesn't sleep mid-walkthrough.
  • Offline Vault — if your connection drops, recordings are stored locally and uploaded automatically when you're back online.
  • Loading Indicator — you'll see an Analyzing indicator during the 20–30 second processing window.

5. The Comps Tab

Comparable sales ("comps") are the foundation of any defensible ARV. The Comps tab lets you pull comps automatically from RentCast, edit them, and add your own.

5.1 Pulling Comps with RentCast

Tap the RentCast button to auto-pull sale comps based on the property's address. RentCast returns:

  • A list of recent comparable sales with addresses, sale prices, sale dates, beds, baths, square footage, and distance.
  • An estimated ARV based on the weighted average of those comps adjusted to your property's square footage.
Property Specs first, then comps

For best comp quality, enter your Property Specs (beds, baths, sqft) BEFORE pulling comps. RentCast uses your specs to weight comps by similarity. A 3/2/1500 home and a 4/3/2500 home are very different — comps that match yours produce much better ARV estimates.

5.2 Editing and Adding Comps Manually

You can edit any auto-pulled comp or add your own. Manual additions are useful when:

  • You know about a sale RentCast missed — for example, a recent off-market flip.
  • You want to remove a comp that doesn't actually match (different school district, busy road, very different layout).
  • You're testing different scenarios for a stress test.

Each comp has fields for address, sale price, beds, baths, square footage, and an editable weight.

5.3 The Weighted ARV Calculation

Comp-Derived ARV is calculated as a weighted price-per-square-foot, multiplied by your subject property's square footage. The weighting accounts for similarity — comps with closer specs and locations contribute more to the final number.

Practical implication: if your subject is 1,800 sqft and your comps are mostly 1,500–2,000 sqft, your ARV will be reliable. If your comps are mostly 2,500+ sqft mansions, the per-sqft number will be inflated and the system can only weight what you give it. Curate your comps.

5.4 Subject Square Footage — Read Only Here

The Comps tab displays your subject property's square footage as a reference, but it cannot be edited here. Edit it on the Deal Analysis tab under Property Specs. This prevents accidental drift between tabs.

6. The Rental Pivot Tab

Sometimes the flip math doesn't quite work — but the rental math does. The Rental Pivot tab gives you a quick rental analysis using rental comps and DSCR (Debt-Service Coverage Ratio).

6.1 Pulling Rental Comps

Tap the RentCast button on this tab to pull rental comps and a fair-market-rent estimate. You'll get a list of nearby rentals with rents, beds, baths, and square footage, plus an estimated monthly rent for your property.

6.2 DSCR and Cash Flow

DSCR is the metric most rental lenders use to underwrite. The formula:

DSCR = (Monthly Rent − Operating Expenses) / Monthly Loan Payment

A DSCR of 1.0 means rent exactly covers your loan and expenses. Most lenders want 1.20 or higher. FlipLogic shows your DSCR live as you adjust rent and expenses.

Cash flow is just (Rent − Expenses − Payment). Positive cash flow means the property pays you each month.

6.3 When to Pivot

Common pivot scenarios:

  • The flip doesn't pencil at your target ROI, but the rental cash flow is strong enough to justify holding.
  • Market is softening — you'd rather collect rent for two years than fire-sale a flip.
  • Property has features (deep lot, ADU potential) that have more value to a long-term hold than a flipper buyer.

7. The Stress Test Tab

The Stress Test tab runs three what-if scenarios on your deal so you can see what happens when reality doesn't go to plan.

7.1 The Three Scenarios

ScenarioWhat It Tests
Best CaseARV slightly higher than expected, rehab on or under budget. The optimistic outcome.
Base CaseYour current numbers as entered — what the deal looks like if everything goes as planned.
Worst CaseARV pulls back, rehab overruns, hold time extends. The pessimistic outcome that you can still survive — or not.

7.2 Deal Health Check

The Deal Health Check is a quick read on how robust your deal is to bad news. A healthy deal still produces an acceptable outcome in the worst case. A fragile deal looks great in the base case but goes underwater the moment anything slips.

Use the Stress Test before committing capital, especially on tight-margin deals.

8. The Scope of Work Tab

The Scope of Work (SOW) tab is where line-item rehab costs live. Items get here either by AI walkthrough findings or by manual entry.

8.1 Adding and Editing Items

Each scope item has:

  • A short description (e.g., "Replace HVAC condenser").
  • A category (Mechanical, Kitchen, Bath, Flooring, Roof, Exterior, etc.).
  • A My Estimate cost.

Items are grouped by category for easy review. The total at the bottom feeds the AI Walkthrough rehab source on the Deal Analysis tab — change a scope item, and your MAO, ROI, and Deal Score update live.

8.2 Adding Items From AI Walkthrough

On the AI Walkthrough tab, every finding has an Add to Scope button. Tapping it pushes the item to this tab with the AI's category and estimate filled in. Edit later if you want.

8.3 Blind SOW Print

The Blind SOW Print feature generates a contractor bid sheet that includes your scope items and categories — but NOT your cost estimates. You hand this sheet to multiple contractors and get bids without anchoring them to your number. Then you compare the bids honestly.

This is the standard workflow used by experienced flippers to get competitive bids. It is built into FlipLogic so you can print or PDF the sheet directly from the app.

9. The Lender Packet Tab

The Lender Packet tab compiles your deal into a professional document ready to send to a hard money lender, partner, or investor.

9.1 What's In the Packet

  • Property summary — address, beds, baths, sqft, year built.
  • Deal financials — purchase price, rehab, ARV, MAO, ROI, profit.
  • Loan request — loan amount, terms, LTV, LTC.
  • Comps — the comps you've pulled, with prices and details.
  • Scope of Work — line items grouped by category.
  • Field notes you've added on the Deal Analysis tab.

9.2 Preview and Print

The packet preview updates live as you change deal data. When you're ready, tap Print — most browsers will let you save as PDF directly from the print dialog. You can do this on desktop or mobile.

10. Concepts Explained

This section is the conceptual primer behind every number FlipLogic shows you. Skim it once now, return to it as questions come up.

10.1 ARV (After-Repair Value)

ARV is what the property will sell for once rehab is done. Everything else in fix-and-flip math depends on ARV. If your ARV is wrong, your offer is wrong, your loan is wrong, and your profit is wrong.

ARV is grounded in comps — recent sales of similar properties in the same neighborhood. The closer the matches (size, condition, location, school), the better the ARV.

10.2 MAO (Maximum Allowable Offer)

MAO is the highest price you can pay and still hit your target margin. The standard formula:

MAO = (ARV × MAO %) − Rehab Cost

The MAO % is your target — typically 70%, lower for tighter markets, higher only when you have other levers (cheap capital, high-confidence ARV, low risk).

If your purchase price is above MAO, you have three levers: negotiate the purchase down, reduce rehab costs, or get more confidence in a higher ARV.

10.3 ROI (Return on Investment)

ROI is your net profit divided by your total project cost, expressed as a percentage. FlipLogic calculates project cost as purchase + rehab + buy-side closing. ROI tells you how efficiently your capital worked.

10.4 LTV (Loan-to-Value)

LTV is your loan amount divided by ARV. Lenders use LTV to size loans — most fix-and-flip lenders will go up to 70–75% LTV. A 65% LTV deal is conservative; an 80% LTV deal will struggle to get financed.

10.5 LTC (Loan-to-Cost)

LTC is your loan amount divided by total project cost. Some lenders cap LTC instead of (or in addition to) LTV. LTC tells you how much of the project the lender is funding versus how much you're bringing to the table.

10.6 DSCR (Debt-Service Coverage Ratio)

DSCR is the rental world's version of "can this property carry itself?" It's monthly net operating income divided by monthly debt service. 1.20 or higher is the typical lender threshold. Below 1.0 means the property doesn't even cover its own loan from rent.

10.7 Multi-Source Switching

Real deals have multiple credible numbers for rehab and ARV. FlipLogic lets you keep all of them visible and switch the active source with a tap. This isn't a quirk — it's the design.

Examples of why you'd switch:

  • Started with an Initial rehab estimate, ran an AI walkthrough — switch to AI Walkthrough.
  • Got a contractor bid that's more reliable than the AI estimate — switch to Manual Override and enter the contractor number.
  • Pulled comps and trust the comp-derived ARV more than your initial guess — the system auto-switches you.

10.8 Deal Score (HOT / WARM / COLD / DEAD)

FlipLogic scores every deal automatically based on three criteria: ROI, LTV, and net profit. Switching active sources changes the score in real time.

ScoreCriteria
HOTROI ≥ 20% AND LTV ≤ 70% AND profit > 0
WARMROI ≥ 12% AND LTV ≤ 80% AND profit > 0
COLDROI ≥ 5% AND profit > 0
DEADAnything below COLD — including any deal that loses money.

HOT is the target. WARM is workable if other factors (location, exit timing, partner financing) tilt your way. COLD is for a closer look — sometimes there's a hidden lever to pull. DEAD is dead — move on.

13. Tips and Best Practices

13.1 Get Specs In First

Before you pull comps or run AI analysis, fill in your Property Specs. Beds, baths, and especially square footage drive everything — comp matching, ARV per-sqft weighting, AI cost estimates. Five minutes of accuracy here saves a lot of confusion later.

13.2 Use Multiple Sources Deliberately

Don't just settle on the first number you got. Set an Initial Estimate, run an AI walkthrough, and pull RentCast comps. Compare. When the three sources agree, your confidence is high. When they disagree, that's a signal to dig deeper before committing capital.

13.3 The 70% MAO Rule Is a Default, Not a Law

70% is the industry standard because it bakes in margin for surprises — and surprises happen. Adjust the MAO percentage when you have a real reason: cheap capital, high-confidence ARV, partner equity, conservative market. Don't adjust it just because a deal doesn't pencil.

13.4 Always Stress Test Tight Deals

If your base-case ROI is 12% (WARM territory), spend a minute on the Stress Test tab before signing anything. A WARM deal can become DEAD with a 5% ARV pullback and a 15% rehab overrun — both of which are common.

13.5 Get Blind Bids

Use the Blind SOW Print feature. Hand the same scope sheet to three contractors. The bids you get back will be more honest, and you'll get a real spread to negotiate from.

13.6 Use the Demo Deal as a Reference

Your account starts with a Demo Deal already loaded. It's pre-populated with sample data and is excluded from the AI trial counter — explore every tab, click every button, and break things on the demo without consequences.

13.7 Field Notes Earn Their Keep Six Months Later

Use the Field Notes section liberally. "Seller is motivated, divorce situation" or "agent said two other offers" or "foundation crack — get inspector" are the kinds of things you forget by the time you're underwriting again next quarter. Future-you will thank present-you.

Does FlipLogic AI work on my phone?

Yes. FlipLogic AI is fully responsive — it works on any modern web browser on iOS or Android, including the AI Walkthrough recording features. It also works on tablets and desktops.

Do I need an Anthropic, Deepgram, or RentCast API key?

No. Those services are integrated into the platform — you don't need any API keys. Your subscription covers usage.

Is my data backed up?

Yes. All deals, comps, scope items, and walkthrough findings are stored in our cloud database with automatic syncing across your devices. If you delete a deal, it is permanently gone — there's no undo button.

Can I share deals with a partner?

Not yet. Deal sharing is on the roadmap as part of a future Coach tier. For now, the Lender Packet (printed or saved as PDF) is the cleanest way to share a deal.

Why does my MAO change when I switch the rehab source?

Because MAO is calculated from your active rehab cost. (ARV × MAO %) − Rehab. If you switch from a $30,000 Initial Estimate to a $42,000 AI Walkthrough total, MAO drops by $12,000. That's the whole point of multi-source switching — your offer adjusts in real time as your rehab understanding firms up.

What happens to my deals if I cancel my subscription?

Your deals stay in your account. You'll keep access through the end of your billing period. After that, your account reverts to free-tier limits (8 stored deals, AI features locked on new deals). Existing deals remain visible — no data is deleted.

Can I use FlipLogic for rentals only?

It's optimized for fix-and-flip, but the Rental Pivot tab gives you DSCR, cash flow, and rental comps — enough to evaluate a buy-and-hold. If your portfolio is rental-only, you'll be using a subset of the app.

How accurate are the AI cost estimates?

They're regionally informed and grounded in property age (year built), but they're estimates. Use them as a starting point. For final numbers, especially on bigger items (kitchens, bathrooms, mechanicals, roofs), get contractor bids.

What if I disagree with a comp RentCast pulled?

Edit it or delete it on the Comps tab. You can adjust the weight, change the price, or remove a comp entirely. Add your own comps too if you know about sales RentCast missed.

Can I export my deals as a spreadsheet?

Not currently — true spreadsheet export and PDF export (beyond the browser print dialog) are on the backlog.

16. Troubleshooting and Getting Help

16.1 Common Issues

My screen went dark while recording — did I lose my walkthrough?

FlipLogic uses a wake lock to keep your screen on during recording. If the screen does sleep (different devices behave differently), the offline vault feature stores your recording locally. When you return to the app, it should sync and process automatically. If you lose work, contact support.

My RentCast comps look wrong — too high, too low, or way off.

Two things to check first:

  1. Are your Property Specs filled in correctly? RentCast weights comps by similarity to your subject. Wrong specs = wrong comps.
  2. Are you in a market with thin recent sales? In rural or low-volume areas, RentCast may pull comps that are technically nearby but not really comparable.

If the comps look bad, edit or delete them on the Comps tab and add manual comps from your own research.

The AI walkthrough returned scope items that don't make sense.

This can happen with very short audio, blurry photos, or video that didn't capture the actual condition. Try:

  • Re-running with longer / clearer audio (audio mode).
  • Re-running with more photos showing each room (photos mode).
  • Switching to Smart Mode for a thorough mixed audio/video walkthrough.

Re-running on a deal you've already used a trial slot on doesn't cost you another trial slot.

I forgot my password and the reset email never arrived.

Check your spam folder. If you've requested several resets in a short window, the email service may have rate-limited the address. Wait an hour and try again. If it still doesn't arrive, email support@fliplogic.ai.

I can't find a deal I created.

Open the sidebar — your deals are listed there in the order they were created. If you don't see it, make sure you're signed in to the same account. If a deal seems to have disappeared, contact support and we'll investigate.

My subscription was charged but the app still shows me on Free Trial.

Subscription updates are pushed live to the app via webhook, but if you navigated away during checkout the app might not have refetched. Try refreshing the page or signing out and back in. If the issue persists, contact support with your transaction date — we can fix it on the back end immediately.
